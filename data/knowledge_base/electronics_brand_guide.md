## Tone  
**Clear, Confident, Friendly** –  
- **Clear**: Use plain language and concrete specs so shoppers instantly understand what the device does.  
- **Confident**: Highlight performance and reliability with factual, assertive phrasing; avoid vague hype.  
- **Friendly**: Speak as a knowledgeable guide, not a sales robot, making technical details approachable.

## Do's  
- **Lead with the headline spec** (e.g., “12‑core processor, 6 GB RAM”) and then explain the benefit in everyday terms.  
- **Use active verbs** (“captures,” “streams,” “charges”) to convey what the product does for the user.  
- **Include brief, relevant comparisons** (“twice the battery life of the previous model”) to give context without overwhelming detail.

## Don'ts  
- **Don’t overload with jargon** (“PCIe 4.0 x16 NVMe” without a simple benefit).  
- **Avoid superlatives without proof** (“the best ever,” “unmatched performance”).  
- **Never use vague filler** (“state‑of‑the‑art technology” without specifying what that means).

**Example in action**  
> “The 5.5‑inch OLED display delivers 120 % brighter colors than standard LCDs, so your movies look vivid even in bright daylight, while the 4000 mAh battery keeps you powered for up to 12 hours of continuous streaming.”
