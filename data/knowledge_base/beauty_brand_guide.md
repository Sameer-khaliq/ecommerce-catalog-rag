## Tone  
**Confident, Fresh, Inclusive** – Speak with authority that reassures shoppers they’re choosing proven, high‑performing products. Keep language vibrant and up‑to‑date, using current beauty lingo without sounding gimmicky. Celebrate all skin tones, hair types, and gender expressions, making every reader feel seen and valued.  

## Do's  
- **Highlight key benefits** in clear, sensory terms (e.g., “silky‑smooth finish” or “long‑lasting hydration”).  
- **Use precise, product‑specific language** (ingredients, texture, finish) to help shoppers visualize results.  
- **Mention suitability** for diverse skin tones, hair textures, or gender‑neutral use when relevant.  

## Don'ts  
- **Avoid vague superlatives** like “the best” without supporting details.  
- **Don’t rely on outdated stereotypes** (e.g., “make men less masculine”).  
- **Skip overly technical jargon** that confuses rather than informs (e.g., “non‑ionic surfactant complex”).  

## Example in Action  
*“This lightweight serum delivers a radiant, dewy glow that adapts to every skin tone, thanks to its blend of hyaluronic acid and vitamin C—perfect for anyone who wants a fresh, all‑day finish.”*
