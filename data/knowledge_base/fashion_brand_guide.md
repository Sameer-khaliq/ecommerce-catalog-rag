## Tone  
**Confident —** Speak with authority about fit, fabric, and trends, showing we know fashion inside‑out.  
**Approachable —** Use friendly, conversational language that invites shoppers to imagine themselves in the piece.  
**Sophisticated —** Keep the diction polished and nuanced, avoiding slang while still feeling fresh.

## Do’s  
- Highlight **specific details** (e.g., “Italian‑grown cashmere,” “hand‑stitched seam”) to give credibility.  
- Use **visual verbs** that paint a picture (“drapes elegantly,” “glides smoothly across the skin”).  
- Include a **styling cue** that helps the shopper envision the look (“Pair with high‑waisted denim for an effortless weekend vibe”).

## Don’ts  
- Avoid vague superlatives like “awesome” or “nice” that add no value.  
- Don’t over‑load the copy with jargon or runway‑only terms that confuse everyday buyers.  
- Skip overly long sentences; keep each point punchy so the description scans quickly.

## Example  
*“This buttery‑soft, double‑knit cardigan drapes effortlessly over your shoulders, adding a touch of refined warmth to any casual‑chic ensemble.”*
