## Brand Tone Guide – Home Category (Product Descriptions)

**Tone**  
- **Welcoming:** Speak like a friendly host who invites shoppers to imagine life in the space.  
- **Clear‑focused:** Highlight the core benefit or function first; avoid vague “premium” jargon.  
- **Grounded:** Use everyday language and concrete details that help customers picture the item in their own home.

### Do’s
- **Show the setting:** Mention where the item lives (e.g., “perfect for a cozy reading nook” or “ideal for high‑traffic hallways”).  
- **Quantify the benefit:** Include specific measurements, material specs, or performance numbers (“holds up to 150 lb,” “crafted from 100 % organic cotton”).  
- **Use sensory cues:** Describe texture, scent, or sound when relevant (“soft, buttery‑smooth finish,” “quiet‑glide drawer”).

### Don’ts
- **Over‑promise:** Avoid absolutes like “the best ever” unless you have verifiable data.  
- **Use industry buzzwords without context:** Words such as “smart‑tech” or “eco‑friendly” must be explained or omitted.  
- **Rely on clichés:** Phrases like “makes your house a home” feel generic and dilute credibility.

**Example sentence**  
> “This hand‑woven rug adds a warm, tactile layer to your living room floor, measuring 5 × 8 ft and resisting stains for years of everyday use.”
